Research Experience

  • Real-Time Image Analysis for Additive Manufacturing (RIAM): This is a collaborative project between the University of Maryland and the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST). The project aims to implement a real-time anomaly detection process in additive manufacturing using unlabeled data with a lower computational cost. The work is also directed to automate the anomaly detection system for edge devices.
  • Real-Time Wireless Communication for Industrial Networks (RWCIN): This is a collaborative project between the University of Maryland and the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST). The project aims to characterize and categorize different aggressors for industrial wireless channels and develop cyberattack datasets for industrial wireless communication. The final plan extends to implementing a real-time cyberattack detection system for edge devices using the well-curated dataset.
  • Maryland–Nebraska Video Analytics for Public Safety (MNVP): This is a collaborative project among the University of Maryland, the University of Nebraska-Lincoln, and the Baltimore Police Department. The project focused on analyzing criminal activities and the positions of firearms using body-worn camera video and audio and contributed to public-safety.
  • Undergraduate Thesis: Automatic Emotion Recognition from EEG Signal in Time-Frequency Domain with Reduced Channels Using Deep Neural Architecture: The project aims to automate the emotion recognition task by exploiting feature-based and deep learning-based approaches with fewer channels using different datasets.
  • Automatic Detection of COVID-19 from Chest X-ray Images:In the study, COVID-19, viral, bacterial pneumonia, and normal cases are diagnosed from chest radiographic images. Different ensembling strategies are employed, and several deep learning architectures are implemented with better accuracy, precision, sensitivity, and specificity for automatic COVID-19 detection.
